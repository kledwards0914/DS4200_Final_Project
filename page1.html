Boxplot: Popularity Distribution by Genre

Take Away:

This boxplot compares popularity score distributions across 15 Spotify genres. Metal and blues stand out with the highest medians (around 67 and 56 respectively) and wide interquartile ranges, suggesting strong but variable performance. Acoustic and mandopop also show high medians with large spreads, indicating a mix of hits and underperformers within the genre. On the lower end, idm, kids, and grindcore are consistently unpopular. Their boxes sit below 20 with little spread, meaning most songs in these genres perform similarly poorly. Latino and folk have the most dramatic whiskers, stretching nearly the full 0–90 range, showing extreme variability from song to song. Notable outliers include a rock-n-roll track scoring around 80 and a latino track near 90, both well above their genre's typical range.

Interaction:

To read this visualization, compare the median line inside each box, the overall height of the box, and any outlier points. The median shows the typical popularity for that genre, while the spread shows how much popularity varies within it. Hover over the box plots to see specific statistics such as the max, Q3, median, Q1, and miniumm of the genre that plot represents. Hover over the individual box plots for additional information.
