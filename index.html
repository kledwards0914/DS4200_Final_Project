Exploring the Relationship Between Audio Features and Popularity in Spotify Music

Introduction:

Hi everyone! We're Kaitlyn, Mia, and Isabelle, and our project is called Exploring the Relationship Between Audio Features and Popularity in Spotify Music. Music streaming platforms like Spotify use audio signals to power their recommendation algorithms. We were curious about what actually makes a song popular — whether that's the genre it belongs to, how much energy it has, how danceable it is, or how consistently artists appear in the dataset. These questions are what drove our project. Our main tasks for designing our visuals were: first, to understand how popularity varies across genres; second, to explore how popularity is distributed across tiers within the top genres; third, to examine the relationship between tempo and danceability across artists and genres; fourth, to see how energy levels differ across the most common genres and whether higher energy correlates with higher popularity; and fifth, to identify which artists are most represented in the dataset. To do this, we used a dataset from Kaggle containing over 114,000 Spotify tracks spanning 125 genres, with audio features like danceability, energy, tempo, loudness, and more, along with metadata like genre, artist, and popularity score.
