Scatter Plot: Tempo and Danceability

Take Away:

This scatter plot explores the relationship between tempo and danceability across genres and artists. Overall, there is no strong linear relationship. You can see that high danceability scores appear at nearly every tempo range, confirming that faster does not mean more danceable. The most interesting outlier is probably a yellow point (latino) near 140 BPM with a danceability of 0.93, sitting above the rest of the chart. A cluster of yellow points also appears around 160–180 BPM with danceabilities of 0.80–0.83, suggesting one artist dominates that high-tempo, high-danceability area. On the opposite end, several dark blue points (black-metal) concentrate in the 100–130 BPM range with danceabilities below 0.2. This reinforces what the boxplot showed about the genre's low popularity. Most genres mix together in the 90–130 BPM area, making tempo alone a poor predictor of danceability.

Interaction:

Use the Artist Dropdown feature to filter the chart. The selected artists stay highlighted while others fade. Hover over any point to see the artist name, tempo, and danceability. The bar chart on the right updates automatically to reflect the genre breakdown of the filtered selection.
